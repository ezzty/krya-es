Up to now, I've only read these books—maybe missing one or two. I'll just make a brief record.

*Wolf Totem* — One of my favorite Chinese novels, highly recommended.

*On the Road* — I love this group of confused young people who seem like madmen, highly recommended.

*Moment in Peking* — Yao Mochou is such an interesting woman, worth reading.

*The Golden Age of the Jin Family* — I like women like Leng Qingqiu, with a touch of sympathy, worth reading.

*Fate in Tears and Laughter* — I've almost forgotten it, average.

*Hawthorn Tree Forever* — Read it long before the movie, then watched the film too; equally flavorful, worth reading.

*Red Poppies* — The description of the fool feeling bubbles coming out of his bones is the most interesting, worth reading.

*In Search of Lost Time* — Didn't finish, too long, and couldn't get through it.

*Walden* — Rereading it, the kind you flip through when you have nothing to do, a keeper.

*Dream of the Red Chamber* — The second read was a bit more interesting than the first, but still didn't finish the second time, recommended.

*Romance of the Three Kingdoms* — Can't say I support any side, like Cao Cao's dominance and Liu Bei's kindness, recommended.

*The Catcher in the Rye* — Holden influenced me to some extent, recommended.

*Grass-like Youth* — Maybe read it again after college; regrettably I read it too early, worth reading.

*Return to Provence* — Didn't understand much at the time, just a record of Provence, worth reading.

*Ordinary World* — XX and XX's love made me sentimental; life in this world is not easy, highly recommended.

*Romeo and Juliet* — Not many people like reading plays, worth reading.

*Hamlet* — Same as above, average.

*Romance of the Western Chamber* — Just because it was in the textbook, I read the whole thing, average.

*Peony Pavilion* — You know the type, average.

*My Name Is Red* — Though it's a Nobel Prize winner, I'm unfamiliar with Middle Eastern culture, quite perplexing, average.

*Fortress Besieged* — One of my favorite Chinese novels, highly recommended.

*Border Town* — Pure love in western Hunan, average.

*The Old Man and the Sea* — The old man conquered the fish, though it was eaten clean by the time he reached shore; dreams don't die, highly recommended.

*The Adventures of Tom Sawyer* — Recommend it to the kids of people who visit my blog; I was young once, average.

*Gulliver's Travels* — Same as above, worth reading.

*Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea* — Same as above, worth reading.

*The Adventures of Robinson Crusoe* — Same as above, worth reading.

*Dong Qiao's Essays* — Forgive me, I didn't understand much back then, average.

*Historical Sky* — Actually, I don't like modern historical novels, really don't, average.

*Norwegian Wood* — Thinking back now, this book feels nostalgic: Watanabe, Naoko, Midori Kobayashi, recommended.

*Lin Qingxuan's Essays* — Love the wind chimes one the most; seems like Taiwanese people always bring Buddhism into things, worth reading.

*Zhou Guoping's Essays* — Lightly touched, no comment.

*The Unbearable Lightness of Being* — If I have time to read it a second time, maybe I'll fully understand it, worth reading.

*The Reader* — Forgot the main plot, only remember reading and making love, worth reading.

*Small World* — A bit like *Fortress Besieged*, but not as witty; reminds me of some beast, worth reading.

*The Horse Whisperer* — Almost forgot the main plot, but I remember it was quite interesting, worth reading.

*The Call* — After reading this, I had the urge to read McCullough's *The Thorn Birds*, but it's too thick, recommended.

*That Summer of Love* — Same kind of story about beast scholars, average.

*The Last Letter from Your Lover* — Some loves you'll never get, but never forget in your heart, recommended.

*Mansion Gate* — Another unfinished novel, no comment.

*Letter from an Unknown Woman* — I love you, you never knew, unless I died, highly recommended.

*1984* — No explanation needed, highly recommended.

*The Little Prince* — Forgive me, I was too childish when reading this, no comment.

*Père Goriot* — These days, who still reads classics? Quoted in a previous post, worth reading.

*To Live* — Unfinished, no comment.

*The Great Gatsby* — No explanation, recommended.

*The Bridges of Madison County* — Too sci-fi, worth reading.

*Tagore's Poetry Collection* — Every piece feels like a classic, recommended.

*Eugénie Grandet* — Forgot, no comment.

*We All Lost on Graduation Day* — Many people lost love that day, worth reading.

*Sadness Flows Upstream* — The only book by the Fourth Miss I've read; regrettably, in high school I didn't have such cool girlfriends, worth reading.

*Charlotte's Web* — That spider's name is Charlotte, recommended.

*Currency Wars* — Mysterious; I somewhat agree with the gold standard, think it could solve economic problems, worth reading.

*Message in a Bottle* — Like *The Last Letter from Your Lover*, a Nicholas Sparks bestseller romance novel; didn't have time to finish, no comment.

*The Kite Runner* — For you, a thousand times over, recommended.

*The Awakening of Design* — Professional book, don't read if you don't like it.

*Xi Murong's Poetry Collection* — Really like her poems, so beautiful, highly recommended.

*Deserted Village Apartment* — Actually not scary, worth reading.

*Half a Lifetime's Romance* — I want you to know there's someone in this world who will wait for you forever, highly recommended.

*Solo* — Out of print, just for collection; not many people regret it, the publisher wisely retreated, average.

*Demi-Gods and Semi-Devils* — If you've seen the TV series, no need to read the novel, recommended.

*The Return of the Condor Heroes* — Same as above, recommended.

*The Heaven Sword and Dragon Saber* — Same as above, average.

*The Legend of the Condor Heroes* — Same as above, recommended.

*White Night* — I just want to walk with you under the sun holding hands; the first few episodes of the Japanese drama of the same name are pretty good, highly recommended.

*The Devotion of Suspect X* — Two books by Higashino, but I prefer *White Night*, recommended.

*Buddha on Line 1* — The true thoughts of a qualified literati, recommended.

*Lang Xianping Says: Why Are Our Days So Hard?* — One of the top ten garbage books of 2010, garbage.

*1Q84* BOOK 1 — Book 3 was spoiled, looking back book 1 feels interesting, planning to reread, no comment.
